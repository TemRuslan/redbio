Technology BIO‑RED

BIO‑RED combines sulfur bio‑oxidation and controlled bioleaching to simultaneously dealkalize red mud and transfer selected metals into solution.

Why red mud matters

Red mud (bauxite residue) is a highly alkaline, polymetallic by-product of alumina production. Its storage creates long-term risks for soil and water, while the same material contains recoverable value.

Hazard profile

High alkalinity and fine particles can cause dust and alkaline leachate issues.

Resource potential

Red mud can contain Al, Fe, Ti and rare earth elements that are relevant for modern supply chains.

What BIO‑RED does

We develop biometallurgical routes that dealkalize red mud and mobilize target metals under controlled reactor conditions.

  • 1 Decrease alkalinity to enable safer handling and downstream utilization.
  • 2 Extract valuable metals (Al, Ce, Y and others) by bio-assisted leaching.
  • 3 Validate results with standard analytics (ICP, SEM, XRD) and publish evidence.

Technology overview

BIO‑RED combines sulfur bio‑oxidation and controlled bioleaching to simultaneously dealkalize red mud and transfer selected metals into solution.

Executive summary

Elemental sulfur (S⁰) is biologically oxidized by sulfur‑oxidizing bacteria (e.g., Sb. thermosulfidooxidans), generating acidity (H⁺). Lower pH enables dissolution of alkaline components and helps mobilize valuable metals.

Mechanism

S⁰ bio‑oxidation → sulfate + H⁺ (biogenic acidity) → pH decrease → dealkalization + metal leaching. [1]

What we control

Aeration, S⁰:RM ratio, temperature, pH/Eh and residence time are tuned to feedstock and target metals.

Evidence & key results

Selected peer‑reviewed findings that support and contextualize the BIO‑RED approach.

Bioleaching in controlled reactors

[1]
  • Reported metal recoveries up to Al 89%, Ce 84%, Y 91% under selected conditions.
  • Effective dealkalization via high dissolution rates of Na/K/Ca (>95%).
  • Monitoring and validation via ICP analysis and solid-phase methods (SEM, XRD, STXM).

Indigenous alkaliphiles as bio-tools

[2]
  • Alkali‑halophilic isolates tolerate pH 7–11 and NaCl up to 3 M.
  • Reported pH neutralization of 10% red mud within 24 h for selected isolates.
  • Genomic analysis (RAST) discusses stress tolerance and metal resistance mechanisms.

Dealkalization methods map

[3]
  • Critical review of red mud hazards and dealkalization routes.
  • Compares water washing, acid neutralization, ion precipitation/replacement and metallurgical methods.
  • Helps position biotechnological approaches within the broader process landscape.
